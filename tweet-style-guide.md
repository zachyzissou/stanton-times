# The Stanton Times - WSJ-Style Tweet Guide

**Goal:** Professional, punchy, authoritative. Think Wall Street Journal meets the frontier.

---

## The WSJ Formula

### 1. Strong Ledes
Lead with the news, not the background. The first sentence should hit.

**âŒ Weak:**
> "CIG has announced that after months of development, the new Alpha 4.6 patch is now available for all players to download and enjoy."

**âœ… Strong:**
> "Alpha 4.6 is live. Server meshing just went from demo to reality."

### 2. No Fluff
Every word earns its place. Cut anything that doesn't add value.

| Cut This | Why |
|----------|-----|
| "It's worth noting that..." | Just note it |
| "As you may know..." | Assume nothing |
| "Interestingly enough..." | Let readers decide |
| "We're excited to report..." | Report, don't emote |
| "At this time..." | Just say "now" |

### 3. Authoritative Tone
We don't hedge unnecessarily. We state facts confidently.

**âŒ Hedgy:**
> "It seems like CIG might be planning to release Pyro sometime soon, possibly in the next update."

**âœ… Authoritative:**
> "CIG targets Pyro for Alpha 4.7. Development enters final sprint."

## Engagement Hooks

### Questions (Use Sparingly)
End major news with a question to prompt replies:

> "Alpha 4.6 drops tomorrow with 47 ship rebalances. Which hull are you checking first?"

> "Server meshing just doubled player caps. Is this the patch that changes everything?"

### Calls to Action
- "Drop your first impressions below."
- "Sound off: is this enough?"
- "Tell us what you're grinding first."
- "Bookmark this threadâ€”we'll update as details emerge."

### Contrarian Takes (Careful)
WSJ isn't afraid to challenge consensus when warranted:

> "Everyone's talking about the new Drake ship. The real story? That buried roadmap update."

## Power Words

### Use More
| Word | Instead of |
|------|------------|
| Live | Available |
| Now | Currently |
| Confirmed | Announced |
| Sources say | Reports indicate |
| Targets | Plans to |
| Breaking | New |
| Slashes | Reduces |
| Surges | Increases |

### Avoid
| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| Exciting | Let readers decide |
| Amazing | Hype language |
| Huge | Overused |
| Insane | Unprofessional |
| Literally | Usually wrong |

## Engagement Metrics to Track

### Primary KPIs
- **Engagement Rate:** (likes + retweets + replies) / impressions
- **Reply Rate:** replies / impressions (conversation starter)
- **Retweet Rate:** retweets / impressions (shareability)

### Benchmarks (News Accounts)
| Metric | Good | Great |
|--------|------|-------|
| Engagement Rate | 2-3% | 5%+ |
| Reply Rate | 0.5% | 1%+ |
| Retweet Rate | 1% | 2%+ |

### What Drives Engagement
1. **Questions** - 2x reply rate
2. **Breaking news** - High retweets
3. **Controversy** - High replies (careful)
4. **Threads** - Extended engagement
5. **Visuals** - 2x overall engagement
